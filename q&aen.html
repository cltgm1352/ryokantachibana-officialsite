Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. Can I bring my pet?
A1. We’re sorry, but pets are not allowed at our accommodation.
Q2. Can I stay alone?
A2. We generally do not accept solo travelers. Priority is given to families with children.
Q3. Is it okay to stay with children?
A3. Absolutely! We warmly welcome families with children and provide a safe and comfortable environment.
Q4. Is there a limit on the number of guests?
A4. The maximum number of guests is 4. If you are traveling with infants, please contact us in advance.
Q5. Is parking available?
A5. Yes, free parking space is available.
Q6. Is there a pick-up service?
A6. We can offer pick-up service to nearby locations such as Urakawa Station with prior arrangement. Please let us know when you book.
Q7. Can I use the kitchen?
A7. Yes, you’re welcome to use the mini kitchen, which includes a rice cooker, microwave, and basic cooking utensils.
Q8. Are meals provided?
A8. Meals are not provided. Please use the kitchen or feel free to ask us about nearby restaurants.
Q9. Is Wi-Fi available?
A9. Yes, free Wi-Fi is available throughout the property.
Q10. What are the check-in and check-out times?
A10. Check-in is from 3:00 PM, and check-out is by 11:00 AM.
Q11. How can I make a reservation?
A11. Reservations are only accepted through Airbnb. Please use the link below to book.
Book Now
Q12. What is your cancellation policy?
A12. Our cancellation policy follows Airbnb’s terms. Please check the reservation page for details.
Q13. Are there any sightseeing spots nearby?
A13. In Urakawa, you can enjoy walks along the Tenryu River, tea field visits, and firefly viewing. Seasonal experiences are also available.
Q14. Can I try local experiences?
A14. Yes, we can introduce you to activities like local tea-making and preserved food workshops upon request.
Q15. Are there places for children to play?
A15. The area around the accommodation is rich in nature, perfect for river play and insect catching.
Q16. How would you describe the accommodation in one sentence?
A16. “A relaxing time for families.” Experience the warmth of family and the nostalgic Japanese countryside in a quiet mountain village.
